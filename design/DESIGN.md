# AI Tinkerers

> Category: Brands

> Surface: web

*The global community for hands‑on AI builders.*

AI Tinkerers creates curated rooms for 116,000+ people shipping real AI systems across 245 cities. Monthly AI meetups with live code demos, hackathons, and technical talks. No slides, no pitches.

## Color Palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| background | Slate 50 | `#f8fafc` | page canvas — measured on body { background-color: #f8fafc }; the site is light, not dark |
| surface | White | `#ffffff` | cards and raised surfaces (bg-white ×65) with 1px slate borders and shadow-sm |
| foreground | Slate ink | `#0f172a` | headlines (text-slate-900) and primary body ink; also the dark footer band (bg-slate-950) |
| muted | Slate 500 | `#64748b` | secondary text, metadata, placeholders (with #475569 for stronger supporting copy) |

| accent-secondary | Indigo bright | `#3d53c2` | accent borders, highlighted chips and link-level emphasis (border-[#3d53c2], bg tints at /10–/20 opacity) |

## Typography
- **Display:** Source Sans Pro — weights 600, 700, 900 — fallbacks: Open Sans, system-ui, -apple-system, Segoe UI, Helvetica Neue, Arial, sans-serif (Hero h1 is text-5xl/6xl font-extrabold tracking-tight; section heads run semibold/bold. Google Fonts now ships this family as 'Source Sans 3'.)
- **Body:** Source Sans Pro — weights 400, 600, 700 — fallbacks: Open Sans, system-ui, -apple-system, Segoe UI, Helvetica Neue, Arial, sans-serif (font-semibold (×235) is the workhorse emphasis weight.)
- **Mono:** ui-monospace — weights 400, 600 — fallbacks: SFMono-Regular, Menlo, Monaco, Consolas, Liberation Mono, Courier New, monospace (System mono stack used for terminal-styled UI (.ait-cc-terminal) and code/telemetry readouts.)

## Voice & Tone

- **Adjectives:** curated, technical, candid, hands-on, no-fluff
- **Tone:** Direct, practitioner-to-practitioner, quietly confident. Short declarative sentences. Evidence over hype: 'Demos, traces, systems, and scars carry more weight than predictions.' Never salesy, never breathless about AI.

### Messaging pillars
- The global community for hands‑on AI builders.
- No slides, no pitches — working systems set the agenda.
- Bring working code, share what broke, and leave with patterns you can ship.
- Always curated: each city protects the room locally while contributing to a shared global standard.
- Like the Homebrew Computer Club — small rooms where practitioners compare notes before the path is obvious.

### Vocabulary
- **Use:** builders, demos, working code, shipping, traces, failure modes, curated rooms, practitioners, patterns you can ship
- **Avoid:** thought leadership, AI panels, sales pitches, revolutionary/game-changing hype, predictions without demos

## Imagery

- **Style:** Real documentary photography from actual meetups — crowded rooms, laptops open, someone demoing at a screen. Warm indoor light, candid framing, no stock photography.
- **Subjects:** builders presenting live demos, audiences in small venues, laptops and projected code, city-by-city event photos
- **Treatment:** Photos sit in rounded-2xl cards with soft slate shadows (0 12px 28px rgba(15,23,42,.12)) and hover scale-105. Playful collage accents: Pangolin handwritten 'sticky-tape' labels on a yellow gradient.
- **Avoid:** stock business imagery, abstract AI brain/robot art, posed corporate group shots

## Layout

- **Radius:** 16px cards (rounded-2xl ×138), full pills for chips/buttons (rounded-full ×337)
- **Border weight:** 1px
- **Spacing:** 8px baseline grid, generous section padding on a centered max-width container

### Posture rules
- Light slate canvas (#f8fafc) with white cards — dark is reserved for the footer band and terminal motifs
- Cards: bg-white + 1px #e2e8f0 border + shadow-sm; hero photos get the deeper 0 12px 28px rgba(15,23,42,.12) shadow
- Chips, tags and secondary buttons are full pills; primary CTAs are solid #31439b with white text
- Accent is used sparingly: indigo buttons and #3d53c2 borders/tints, never large washes
- One playful flourish: Pangolin handwritten sticky-tape labels over photo collages
